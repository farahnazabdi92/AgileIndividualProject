AI Reflection Log

This page documents AI-assisted work, evaluates reliability, and records corrections.

Purpose and Context

This log documents how AI tools were used during the SkillTrack Learning Portfolio project. It focuses on specific prompts, what the AI produced, how the outputs were refined, and where human judgment was required. The goal is to show responsible use, not delegation of understanding.

All AI suggestions were reviewed for accuracy, alignment with the rubric, and explainability in a live demo.

AI Use Case 1

Use Case: Requirements Mapping and Project Planning

Prompt focus: Extract every requirement from the brief and rubric, then create a locked checklist and risk analysis.

What the AI produced

  • Structured requirement lists for each page and feature.
  • Assessment expectations split by pass vs excellent bands.
  • A risk-based priority ranking to guide later phases.

What was changed or refined

  • Removed any speculative requirements not stated in the brief.
  • Aligned the checklist order to the final project flow.
  • Condensed overlapping rubric points to avoid redundancy.

Why it helped: The AI accelerated the initial mapping, but manual review was required to ensure accuracy and prevent scope creep.

Limitations noted: Early drafts were overly verbose and included a few inferred requirements that needed removal.

AI Use Case 2

Use Case: Tutorial and Quiz Design Planning

Prompt focus: Plan the tutorial learning objectives, quiz coverage, and the overall assessment flow without writing final content.

What the AI produced

  • Learning outcomes for HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • A balanced coverage map for quiz topics.
  • Suggested structure for the interactive demo and validation rules.

What was changed or refined

  • Trimmed advanced topics to stay within scope.
  • Aligned quiz difficulty to the tutorial depth.
  • Clarified wording to match the project's instructional tone.

Why it helped: The AI helped maintain consistency between the tutorial and quiz so the assessment feels fair and aligned to the learning outcomes.

Limitations noted: Some early suggestions were generic and needed customization to the SkillTrack concept.

Subtle Error Analysis

Example: Missing-Question Labels After Randomization

Issue: An early AI-generated approach labeled missing questions using the original data order. Because the quiz randomizes question order at runtime, this would have listed incorrect question numbers in validation feedback.

How it was detected: During manual review, the validation list did not match the on-screen order of randomized questions.

Correction: The mapping was changed to use the rendered (shuffled) order when numbering missing questions.

Lesson: Even small logic choices can cause subtle user-facing errors. AI output must be tested against actual runtime behaviour, not just read for plausibility.

Critical Evaluation

Reliability, Risk, and Ethics

  • AI can accelerate planning, but it can also introduce scope creep or vague statements.
  • Every AI suggestion must be checked against the rubric to avoid missing requirements.
  • Over-reliance on AI risks producing code the student cannot explain during the demo.
  • Ethical use requires transparency about AI assistance and clear human ownership of work.
  • Verification and testing are essential because AI does not run or validate the project.

Summary and Learnings

AI was most valuable for planning, structuring, and checking alignment with the rubric. Human judgment was still required to adjust scope, correct subtle logic issues, and ensure explainable outcomes. The final responsibility for accuracy, ethics, and demonstration readiness remains with the student.
